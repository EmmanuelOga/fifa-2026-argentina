## The big mover: the money probe turns inward

For runs on end the financial thread was pure syndication of a fixed corpus. This
pass it moved, and in a specific, verifiable direction: **from interviewing outside
witnesses to reconstructing the scheme from the inside.** All alleged, all unproven.

- **La Nación (Jul 13–14, "El FBI busca reconstruir desde adentro cómo operaba la
  AFA")** reports FBI agents and federal prosecutors reaching **former AFA insiders**,
  not just outside witnesses. Named for the first time: a **former executive
  president of the AFA Consejo Federal**, described in reporting as a lieutenant of
  treasurer Pablo Toviggino. Per La Política Online, this figure has held **several
  informal videoconferences** with US authorities — explicitly framed as informal
  dialogues, **not sworn testimony, and not (yet) a formal cooperating witness.** That
  distinction matters: the H2 changeMind trigger ("a grand jury subpoena, an
  indictment, or a cooperating witness") is **still not met.**
- **Three federal prosecutors named** across two districts: two in Washington DC and
  one in the Southern District of Florida (single-thread sourcing to La Nación —
  attributed, not asserted; names not needed for the site's purposes so I refer to
  the districts, not the individuals).
- **New alleged mechanism:** cash moved to Argentina via informal currency traders
  ("cuevas") / suitcases. Layered on the unchanged $260M / ~$57M-without-clear-
  purpose / five-named-banks / ~$90M-flagged facts.
- **Perceived-target shift:** AFA sources quoted in the Argentine press now describe
  **Toviggino, not Tapia**, as the probe's principal focus, and note he deliberately
  did not travel to the US. Characterization from AFA sources, not verified
  prosecutorial fact — kept at that level.
- **Separate domestic "mansion" case moved** (distinct from the ~$12.7M
